Shakshuka

Description

One of my all-time first-date dishes. Orginally, breakfast in middle east, especially famous in Israel! As well as in Berlin for now… But it's great. Taste it and you'll have to admit that it's one of the most decadent breakfasts you ever had! I often prepare it for dinner. Serve with bread and enjoy your guests' overwhelmed gaze!

Ingredients

Base recipe

  • Olive oil
  • 1 red onion; dashed
  • 1 red pepper; cubed
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 can of dashed tomatoes
  • 1 chili; sliced
  • 2 table spoons of tomato paste
  • salt and pepper

Serve with

  • White bread
  • Labneh or greek yoghurt

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil to medium heat in a large sized pan
  2. Sauté the onion until golden
  3. Add in the chili
  4. Throw in the red pepper and cook until it's getting a little soft
  5. Add in the can of dashed tomatoes
  6. Let it simmer and add in salt and pepper
  7. When the sauce is getting thicker add in the eggs. Therefore:
    1. Create a molde on the tomato-pepper sauce
    2. Carefully, let an egg sink into the mold you just created
    3. If you like, mix the egg white and the tomato sauce
    4. Carefully! Don't destroy the egg yolk
  8. When done for all eggs cover the pan so the egg can set
  9. Per person, arange sauce, an egg and bread and labneh/yoghurt on a plate
  10. Enjoy!
